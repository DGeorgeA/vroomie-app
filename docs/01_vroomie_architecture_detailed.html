Section 01

Executive Summary

What is Vroomie?

Vroomie is an AI-powered vehicle health diagnostics platform that transforms any smartphone into a professional-grade engine sound analyser. By leveraging the device microphone and on-device machine learning (TensorFlow.js YAMNet), Vroomie performs real-time acoustic anomaly detection — identifying faults such as alternator bearing failure, piston knock, intake leaks, and starter motor issues — without requiring any OBD-II hardware or workshop equipment.

Who Is It For?

Vehicle owners, fleet managers, independent mechanics, and roadside assistance operators who need instant, zero-hardware engine diagnostics. The PWA runs on any modern browser — iOS Safari, Android Chrome, or desktop — with no app store download required.

Key Differentiators

🧠 On-Device ML

YAMNet audio classification runs entirely in the browser via TensorFlow.js. Zero server-side inference. Zero latency. Full offline capability after initial model download.

🎙️ Acoustic Fingerprinting

Reference fault patterns are pre-embedded as 1024-dimensional YAMNet vectors and matched via cosine similarity — similar to Shazam's audio fingerprinting approach.

⚡ Real-Time Pipeline

Dual AudioContext architecture separates ML inference (16 kHz) from waveform visualisation (native rate), ensuring smooth 60fps rendering alongside continuous classification.

📱 Progressive Web App

Installable PWA with service worker pre-caching, offline analysis, and push notification readiness. Deployed via GitHub Pages with CDN-backed static delivery.

Cosine Similarity

The core distance metric for comparing live audio embeddings against reference fingerprints:

cos(A, B) = (A · B) / (‖A‖ × ‖B‖) = Σ(Aᵢ × Bᵢ) / (√Σ(Aᵢ²) × √Σ(Bᵢ²))

Implemented as a tight loop over 1024 dimensions in calculateCosineSimilarity(). No external library dependency.

Threshold Rationale

Production Threshold: 0.75
Real-world smartphone microphones introduce environmental noise, room reverb, and non-uniform frequency response. A threshold of 0.75 balances sensitivity (catching real faults) with precision (avoiding false positives from ambient noise).
Why Not 0.90?
Testing showed 0.90 rejects virtually all real-world recordings. Clean WAV references score ~0.95+ against themselves, but live mic audio consistently tops out at 0.78–0.85 against the same references due to acoustic channel differences.

Dual AudioContext Architecture

A single getUserMedia MediaStream is shared between two completely independent AudioContext instances. This separation is critical: YAMNet requires exactly 16 kHz input, while the waveform display benefits from the device's native sample rate (typically 44.1 or 48 kHz).

Why two AudioContexts? The Web Audio API does not allow a single AudioContext to operate at two different sample rates simultaneously. The ML context must resample to 16 kHz for YAMNet compatibility, while the waveform context must remain at native rate for accurate time-domain display. Both are created from the same MediaStream, which the browser multiplexes internally.

Fault Type Reference Library

Complete FAULT_NATURE_LIBRARY from audioMatchingEngine.js. Each entry provides clinical-grade descriptions for the AnalysisDetails report.

KeyNature DescriptionRecommended Fix
alternator_bearing_fault_critical
critical		 The alternator's internal bearing race is failing, generating a sustained high-frequency whine (4–8 kHz). Causes increasing electrical load instability, progressive battery drain, and risk of complete loss of charging while driving. Replace alternator bearing or full assembly. Inspect drive belt for glazing or cracking caused by failing bearing's irregular drag.
BearingAlternator
high		 Severe alternator bearing degradation with high kurtosis in the 4–10 kHz band, indicating metal-on-metal contact within bearing races. Leads to total alternator seizure and stranded vehicle. Replace alternator immediately. Do not operate under high electrical load (A/C, headlights) until repaired.
intake_leak_low
low		 Vacuum or air leak in intake manifold/plenum gasket introducing unmetered air. Causes lean fuel condition, misfires under load, and rough idling. Prolonged lean running risks piston and valve damage. Smoke machine or propane enrichment test to locate leak. Replace intake manifold gasket, vacuum lines, or affected component. Reset ECU fuel trims.
misfire_detected_medium
medium		 One or more cylinders failing to complete combustion reliably. Common causes: fouled spark plugs, weak ignition coils, or injector deposits. Unburnt fuel damages catalytic converter. Cylinder-balance test via OBD-II. Replace spark plugs, inspect coil-on-plug units, run injector cleaning cycle.
MotorStarter
high		 Electrical transient consistent with starter motor engagement or relay chatter. May indicate weak battery, corroded terminals, or failing solenoid. Repeated no-start accelerates flywheel ring gear wear. Load-test battery, inspect terminal corrosion. Test starter solenoid draw with clamp ammeter. Replace battery or starter as indicated.
Piston
high		 Deep low-frequency knocking consistent with piston slap or big-end bearing wear. Piston slap typically cold-onset; bearing knock is constant and load-sensitive. Indicates significant internal engine wear. Oil pressure test and cylinder leak-down test. Measure big-end bearing clearances. Engine rebuild/replacement likely if clearances exceed spec.
water_pump_failure_critical
critical		 Water pump impeller/shaft bearing failing, causing irregular coolant flow. Leads to localised hot spots in cylinder head. Head gasket failure and engine overheating within minutes. Replace water pump assembly and thermostat. Flush cooling system, inspect coolant contamination. Check head gasket.
timing_chain_rattle_high
high		 Timing chain tensioner has lost hydraulic pressure or chain stretched beyond tolerance. Metallic rattle during cold starts/acceleration. Failure causes severe valve-to-piston collision. Replace timing chain, tensioner, and guide rails as complete kit. Use manufacturer-spec engine oil.
PowerSteeringPump
high		 Power steering pump producing abnormal tonal whine under load. Indicates low fluid, cavitating pump, or failing vane mechanism. Loss of power assist occurs suddenly. Check/top up PS fluid. Inspect for leaks at pump, rack, hose fittings. Replace pump and flush fluid if whine persists.
RockerArmAndValve
high		 Rhythmic high-frequency tapping in valvetrain. Consistent with excessive rocker arm clearance, collapsed lifter, or sticking valve stem. Causes camshaft lobe wear and valve float at high RPM. Check valve clearances, adjust to spec. Inspect lifters for hydraulic collapse. Replace worn rockers, pushrods, or lifter assembly.
SerpentineBelt
medium		 Irregular broadband noise in belt drive system. Glazed, cracked, or misaligned serpentine belt. Failure causes simultaneous loss of alternator, power steering, and water pump drive. Inspect belt for cracking, glazing, proper tension. Check all driven pulleys for bearing noise/wobble. Replace belt and tensioner together.

Key Thresholds & Configuration

ParameterValueSource FilePurpose
ANOMALY_THRESHOLD 0.75 mlEmbeddingEngine.js Cosine similarity score ≥ this triggers anomaly detection. Balances sensitivity vs. false positive rate for real-world mic audio.
RMS silence gate 0.01 audioFeatureExtractor.js RMS below this over the full 1-second window rejects the frame as silence — prevents YAMNet inference on empty audio.
Silence rejection ratio 0.50 (50%) AudioRecorder.jsx If more than half of all classification windows were silence-rejected, the recording is aborted — "Unable to detect".
Classification interval 900ms audioFeatureExtractor.js Wall-clock minimum between classification attempts. Prevents overlapping YAMNet inference calls.
ScriptProcessor buffer 4096 samples audioFeatureExtractor.js onaudioprocess block size. At 16 kHz = 256ms per block, ~4 blocks fill the 1-second ring buffer.
Target sample rate 16,000 Hz audioFeatureExtractor.js YAMNet's required input sample rate. AudioContext created with explicit sampleRate: 16000.

Waveform smoothing 0.0 AudioRecorder.jsx Zero temporal smoothing — ensures instant visual response to audio transients.
Auto-gain max 8x AudioWaveform.jsx Maximum amplification for quiet signals. Formula: min(128 / max(maxDev, 4), 8)
